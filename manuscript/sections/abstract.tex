Reactive autoscaling is easy to deploy but often responds too late. This study evaluates a reproducible predictive alternative on public Alibaba traces using aggregate, machine, transfer, and broad service-level evidence. Leakage-free forecasting shows that linear regression is the strongest average point baseline on the aggregate series (MAE 3.514), while \texttt{UA-MSTCN-Lite} still supplies usable $P90$ coverage of 0.920, 0.867, and 0.868 for the 1-, 5-, and 10-minute horizons, respectively. A 12-machine cohort and zero-shot transfer confirm that simple linear structure remains hard to beat for point prediction. A broad audited service cohort of 139 \texttt{app\_du} groups changes the service-level headline: linear regression becomes the strongest average point forecaster (MAE 32.571), while predictive control raises mean SLA violation from 0.017 to 0.031 and scaling actions from 249.0 to 399.4 versus reactive control; over-provisioning falls from 56.31 to 53.68 but is not stable under service bootstrap. Public traces therefore support reproducible predictive-autoscaling evaluation, multi-granularity evidence, and service-level boundary mapping, not a blanket superiority claim.
